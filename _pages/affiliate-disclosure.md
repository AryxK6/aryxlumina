  <section>
    <h2>What Affiliate Links Are</h2>
    <p>An affiliate link is a tracked URL connected to an affiliate program. When you click one of these links and make a purchase on the destination site, we receive a small commission from the retailer. The price you pay is the same whether you use an affiliate link or go directly to the site yourself.</p>
    <p>This is a common and legal way for content websites to earn revenue without charging readers directly for access to articles and guides.</p>
  </section>

  <section>
    <h2>Our Editorial Standards</h2>
    <p>Affiliate relationships do not change what we write. The products and topics we cover are chosen because we think they are genuinely useful to our readers, not because a brand is paying us to feature them.</p>
    <p>We do not write positive reviews in exchange for commissions. If something does not work well, we say so. A product can have an affiliate link and still receive a critical or mixed review from us. The two things are separate.</p>
    <p>We do not accept payment to write sponsored content that is disguised as a regular article. When something is sponsored, it is labeled as such.</p>
  </section>

    <h2>Identifying Affiliate Links</h2>
    <p>We try to make it easy to know when a link might be an affiliate link. You may see phrases like "this is an affiliate link" or "we may earn a commission" near product links. In some cases the disclosure is at the top of the article rather than next to every individual link.</p>
    <p>If you are ever unsure whether a link is an affiliate link, feel free to <a href="/contact-us">contact us</a> and ask.</p>

We take this seriously and have made it a deliberate policy not to let affiliate relationships influence what we write or recommend.</p>
