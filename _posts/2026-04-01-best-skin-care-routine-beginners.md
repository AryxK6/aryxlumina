Starting a skin care routine does not have to be complicated. Most beginners get overwhelmed by the number of products out there, but the truth is, a simple routine with the right steps works better than a 10-step one done inconsistently.

## Why a Basic Routine Matters

Your skin has a natural barrier that protects it from pollution, bacteria, and moisture loss. A good routine keeps that barrier healthy. When you skip cleansing or skip moisturizer, that barrier breaks down, and you start seeing dryness, breakouts, or oiliness.

## The 3 Core Steps

### 1. Cleanser

Wash your face twice a day, morning and night. Use a gentle, fragrance-free cleanser. Harsh soaps strip your skin's natural oils and cause more problems than they solve.

### 2. Moisturizer

Apply moisturizer right after cleansing while your skin is still slightly damp. This locks in hydration. Even oily skin needs moisture. When skin is dehydrated, it produces more oil to compensate.

### 3. Sunscreen (Morning Only)

This is the single most important step for anti-aging and skin health. Use SPF 30 or higher every morning, rain or shine. UV damage is cumulative and shows up years later as dark spots and wrinkles.

## What to Avoid as a Beginner

Do not buy too many products at once. Introduce one new product at a time so you can tell what works and what causes a reaction. Avoid anything with heavy fragrance, especially if you have sensitive skin.

## Building Up Over Time

Once your basic three-step routine feels natural, you can add a vitamin C serum in the morning for brightening, or a retinol product at night for anti-aging. But give your skin at least four to six weeks before judging whether something is working.

Consistency matters more than expensive products. A simple routine done daily will always outperform an elaborate one done occasionally.
